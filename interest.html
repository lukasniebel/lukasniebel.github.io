My research interests lie in studying linear and nonlinear partial differential equations arising in kinetic theory. During my PhD, I conducted research on the regularity theory of kinetic linear equations related to the (fractional) Kolmogorov equation \begin{equation} \tag{1} \begin{cases}\partial_t u + v \cdot \nabla_x u = -(-\Delta_v)^{\beta/2} u + f \\ u(0) = g \end{cases} \end{equation} and (non-) linear variants. Together with my advisor Prof. Rico Zacher, we characterize the existence of unique strong $L^p$-solutions to (1) in terms of function spaces for the data $f$ and $g$. This allows for studying a wide class of nonlinear kinetic PDE. Moreover, we study a priori HÃ¶lder-estimates such as the kinetic analog of the celebrated De Giorgi-Nash-Moser theory. We apply these findings to study $L^p$-solutions of nonlinear kinetic equations and aim to apply the findings to more sophisticated equations such as the Boltzmann and Landau equation in the future. I briefly describe my scientific work in the following.

Kinetic Maximal $L^p$-Regularity

In [N., Zacher 2021 and 2022] we introduce the notion of kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity. Let $A$ be a linear operator with domain $D(A)$ in some Banach space $X$ of functions depending on $(x,v)\in \mathbb{R}^{2d}$. Let further $p\in (1,\infty)$ and $T>0$. We say that $A$ admits kinetic maximal $L^p((0,T);X)$-regularity if for all $f\in L^p((0,T);X)$ there exists a unique distributional solution $u\in L^p((0,T);X)$ of the problem \begin{equation} \begin{cases}\partial_t u + v \cdot \nabla_x u -Au=f,\quad t\in (0,T),\, x,v\in \mathbb{R}^d \\ u(0) = 0,\quad x,v\in \mathbb{R}^d, \end{cases} \end{equation} such that $\partial_t u + v \cdot \nabla_x u\in L^p((0,T);X)$ and $Au \in L^p((0,T);X)$. In [N., Zacher 2022] we have been mainly working with $X = L^q(\mathbb{R}^{2d})$ for some $q \in (1,\infty)$, which corresponds to strong $L^p$-solutions. Consider $A = -(-\Delta_v)^{\beta/2}$ for $\beta \in (0,2]$, then $A$ admits kinetic maximal $L^p(L^q)$-regularity.
Besides the property of kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity, which takes care of the right-hand side $f$ in the kinetic equation, it is also crucial to characterise the trace space of the problem, i.e. to find (regularity) conditions on the initial value $g$, which are necessary and sufficient for the corresponding solution of the problem (with $f=0$) to lie in the described kinetic maximal $L^{p}((0,T);L^q(\mathbb{R}^{2d}))$-regularity class. Using methods from Harmonic analysis we were able to characterise the trace space for the Kolmogorov equation (1) with $\beta\in (0,2]$ in terms of anisotropic Besov spaces. Combined with the kinetic maximal regularity property, this leads to the following characterisation. Setting $Y:=L^{p}((0,T);L^q(\mathbb{R}^{2d}))$ problem (1) possesses a unique solution \begin{equation} u\in Z:=\big\{u\in Y:\,\partial_t u + v \cdot \nabla_x u\in Y\;\mbox{and}\;(-\Delta_v)^\frac{\beta}{2} u\in Y\big\} \end{equation} if and only if $f\in Y$ and $g$ belongs to the anisotropic Besov space \begin{equation} X_{\gamma}:=B_{qp,x}^{\frac{\beta}{\beta+1}(1-\frac{1}{p})}(\mathbb{R}^{2d})\cap B_{qp,v}^{{\beta}(1-\frac{1}{p})}(\mathbb{R}^{2d}). \end{equation} Moreover, the solution space $Z$ embeds continuously into the space $C([0,T];X_{\gamma})$, which in particular means that the solutions of (1) with $f=0$ and $g\in X_{\gamma}$ define a semi-flow in the trace space $X_{\gamma}$.
We extend these results in several directions such as different choices for the base space $X$, i.e. anisotropic Sobolev spaces of possibly negative order and Lebesgue spaces with polynomial weights in $x$ and $v$. Moreover, we introduce temporal weights of the form $t^{1-\mu}$ with $\mu \in (1/p,1]$, which allows considering initial values with less regularity and observing instantaneous regularisation.
Clearly, the fractional Laplacian in velocity is not the only operator with the property of kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity and more complicated operators can be treated. For example $A = a(t,x,v) \colon \nabla_v^2$, where $a$ is an elliptic, bounded and measurable map of symmetric matrices. If $a(t,x,v)$ or $(t,x,v) \mapsto a(t,x+tv,v)$ is uniformly continuous, then $A$ admits kinetic maximal $L^p(L^q)$-regularity. This can be extended to coefficients, which are not necessarily uniformly elliptic. In [N. 2022] we consider a fractional Laplacian with variable density, i.e. \begin{equation*} [A u](t,x,v) = \mathrm{p.v.} \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} ({u(t,x,v+h)-u(t,x,v)}) \frac{a(t,x,v,h)}{|h|^{d+\beta}} \mathrm{d} h. \end{equation*}

Nonlinear Kinetic Equations

The precise solution theory given by kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity allows to study quasilinear kinetic problems of the form \begin{equation} \tag{2} \begin{cases} \partial_t u +v \cdot \nabla_x u = A(u) u + F(u) \\ u(0) = g. \end{cases} \end{equation} Assume that $A(g)$ admits kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity and the mappings $A, F$ satisfy a local Lipschitz condition. Then, there exists a short-time solution to (5). In particular, solutions depend continuously on the initial value and define a semi-flow. This has been proven in [N., Zacher 2022] in a very general setting.
In [N., Zacher 2022] we prove short time existence of solutions to a family of quasilinear kinetic diffusion problems with $A(u) = \nabla_v (a(u) \nabla_v u) $ and suitable $a \colon \mathbb{R} \to (0,\infty)$. The local mass plays an important role in kinetic equations. In this view the following kinetic toy model \begin{equation*} \begin{cases} \partial_t u +v \cdot \nabla_x u = M(u) \, \Delta_v u \\ u(0) = g. \end{cases} \end{equation*} with $M(u)(t,x) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^d} u(t,x,v) \, \mathrm{d} v$ is interesting. We prove short time existence of solutions and obtain global solutions conditional on bounds of the local mass.
