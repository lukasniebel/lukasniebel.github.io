Kinetic maximal \(L^p\)-regularity

Together with Rico Zacher I am working on the $L^p$-regularity theory of linear kinetic PDE such as the fractional Kolmogrovo equation
$\begin{cases} \partial_t u +v \cdot \nabla_x u = -(-\Delta_v)^{\beta/2} u + f \\ u(0) = g \end{cases}$

with $\beta \in (0,2)$ and data $f$ and $g$. We characterize the existence of unqiue solutions in terms of conditions on the data $f$ and $g$ and call this property Kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity. A nice property of this ansatz is that solutions form a semi-flow, in particular, $g \in X_\gamma$ implies $u(t) \in X_\gamma$. Kinetic maximal $L^p$-regularity of the Kolmogorov equation can be extended to related equations with variable coefficients and even more general diffusion operators. We are mainly interested in variants of Lebesgue spaces, including $L^p$ spaces with temporal and spatial weights.

Quasilinear kinetic PDE

The findings on the $L^p$-theory of linear kinetic PDE can be applied to study quasilinear equations. For example one can easily show the existence of a short time solution to the kinetic toy model
$ $
$\begin{cases} \partial_t u +v \cdot \nabla_x u = \rho(u) \Delta_v u\\ u(0) = g \end{cases}$

with $\rho(u) = \int_{\mathbb{R}^n} \, u \mathrm{d}v $ in weighted $L^p$-spaces. We aim to apply this method to more complicated nonlinear kinetic PDE.

A priori estimates for kinetic PDE

Studying solutions of nonlinear kinetic PDE, a priori estimates such as Harnack inequalities turn out to be very useful. A Harnack inequality for the Kolmogorov equation has been proven very recently. We are interested in applying Harnack inequalities, and similar a priori estimates to understand the behavior of solutions to nonlinear kinetic PDE.
